About

scheibo.com is Kirk Scheibelhut’s /shy-bull-hut/ blog dedicated to programming and technology. It is the sister site to kirkscheibelhut.com which is more of a personal blog with a bias towards athletic endeavours.

Most of my code is available on Github. I sometimes sign tags or emails with my public key. My resume is located here, and is likely to be far more up to date than whatever is on my LinkedIn profile.

There is no commenting system enabled on the blog – partly to avoid dealing with spam but mainly to avoid flames and thus allow myself to indulge the belief that people enjoy my site. If you wish to comment, see the contact page for how to reach me.

This site is built on a Jekyll, more specifically jekyll_ext. The backend relies on the extensions found in rfelix’s my_jekyll_extensions, and the front end is heavily ‘inspired by’ (stolen from) Jeff Kreeftmeijer's beautiful site, jeffkreeftmeijer.com. The background image was found on Josh Liba’s Flickr, and is licensed under Creative Commons.

The site was built after a sort of ‘meta–analysis’ of the various sites posted on Jekyll’s Sites page. The result of this work has been extracted to static, the skeleton of my blogs. It features some nice extensions and rake tasks that make designing a Jekyll site easy… especially if you hope to make a Jekyll site that looks exactly like this. As Pablo Picasso said, “Good artists copy, great artists steal.”
